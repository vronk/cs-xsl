Dictionaries

Data

All entries in the dictionaries which are not further indicated are taken from data provided by the collaborator(s) cited on the front page of each dictionary. Data extracted from other published sources is indicated as such and includes the respective reference.

Transcription

The transcription follows the system which is widely accepted in Arabic studies and also used in reference works such as the Encyclopedia of Arabic Language and Linguistics (Leiden: Brill, 2006-2009). For the future it is planned to provide the data in IPA-transcription too.

Following a widespread convention in Arabic dialectology, the data is presented in in a broad phonological transcription that does not indicate allophones.

Arabic roots

In order to facilitate cross-dialectal comparative search all word roots are have to be taken as being strictly etymological, i.e. they reflect the corresponding roots of Standard Arabic wherever possible. The source of reference is the Dictionary of Modern Written Arabic by Hans Wehr (English version edited by J. Milton Cowell, Wiesbaden: Harrassowitz, 1979). For words which have entered Arabic from other languages a (in many hypothetical) root consisting of the mere consonantal structure of the word in question is given.

Part-of-speech

As for verbs a subcategory indicating the so-called verbal form is provided (using the traditional order labelled by Roman numerals from I to X, in case of quadriliteral roots Iq and IIq).

In many cases a clear distinction between adjective and active or passive participle is not possible. If a given lemma is mainly used in an adjectival sense it is labelled as such but with an additional category that indicates the word is morphologically seen a participle.

The gender of Arabic nouns is not noted unless it is contradictory to the rules of Arabic morphology (which is particularly the case with unmarked feminine nouns).
