Şanlıurfa

Official name الرها ar-Ruhā (no longer in use)
Local name urfa ~ al-midīne
Stephan Procházka

Typology

Arabia › Northern Group › Syro-Mesopotamian fringe

Bedouin dialect, minority variety

General

Because the official language of the region is Turkish, the Arabic dialect of the city of Urfa is a minority language. Similar varieties of Arabic are spoken in many villages and smaller towns (e.g. Harran and Akçakale) situated between Urfa and the Syrian border. Urfa Arabic is linguistically very close to the dialects of the sheep and goat breeding nomads (the so-called Shawāya) of the Syrian Steppe and arguably constitutes the northernmost variety of the Arabian Bedouin dialects. Tribal structures among the local Arabic speakers (estimated to be around 150,000) are still very strong, and the relatively strict endogamy results in a high proportion of first language speakers even among the youngest generation. Urfa Arabic is usually not written; but there are internet sites containing phrases, songs, and proverbs in vernacular Arabic written in Latin script.

Research History

Although the existence of Arabic speaking groups in the region has been known since at least the 19th century, the dialect is still poorly documented. A very short grammatical sketch is Procházka 2003, three texts with English translation are found in Procházka 2013, and a comparison of Urfa Arabic with other peripheral dialects of the region is Procházka 2006.
